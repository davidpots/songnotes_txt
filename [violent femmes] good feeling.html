Guitar Chords Used

Here's how to play the guitar chords used in this song. With the exception of a few uses of E-minor, you can get away with only needing D, G, and A-major for the entire song. The difficult part is playing / strumming these relatively few chords in a way that does justice to the sparse intimacy of the song.

Walk-down Transition from Solo to Verse

Here's how I play the transition from the solo/instrumental section back to the verse -- mainly, the walkdown. You can do it in a few different ways. One approach is maintaining the A-chord (barre it with your index finger) and use your pinky and ring fingers to walk the bassnote down:

e-|-----------------------------|---2--------0------------
B-|-------2-----2-----2-----2---|---3--------2------------
G-|-------2-----2-----2-----2---|---2--------2------------
D-|-------2-----2-----2-----2---|---0--------2------------   etc
A-|----0------------------------|------------0------------
E-|----------3-----2-----0------|-------------------------
       A    /G    /F#   /E          D        A 
                                       "Good feeling..."

Alternatively, you can change the chords as you walk down and go from A-G-F#m-Em leading back to the D-chord that starts the next verse. Take your pick.

e-|------0-----3-----2-----0---|---2--------0-----------
B-|------2-----0-----2-----0---|---3--------2-----------
G-|------2-----0-----2-----0---|---2--------2-----------
D-|------2-----0-----4-----2---|---0--------2-----------   etc
A-|----0-------2-----4-----2---|------------0-----------
E-|----------3-----2-----0-----|------------------------
       A     G     F#m   Em        D        A 
                                       "Good feeling..."
